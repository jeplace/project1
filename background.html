Background

From the Fallout fandom wiki:

"The setting of the Fallout series is focused on a "what-if" scenario (referred to as the Divergence) in which the United States of America, following World War II, spearheaded a decades-long socioeconomic shift towards nuclear fusion as a power source, resulting in the country's evolution into a hegemony with a lessened reliance on petroleum by the latter half of the 21st century. By the 2060s, global affairs deteriorated as Earth's non-renewable resources began to deplete and whole nations were cracking under the strain, leading to the Resource Wars to acquire the few sources remaining. In particular, conflict over an oil drilling site in the Pacific Ocean aggravated tensions between the United States and the communist government of China, escalating into the full-fledged Sino-American War that lasted for around a decade up to 2077, when both countries resorted to a nuclear exchange on October 23, an event that became known as the Great War.

In the years leading up to the Great War, massive underground Vaults were constructed across the continental USA by the Vault-Tec Corporation, supposedly to protect the populace from the dangers of fallout in the event of nuclear war. Although a total of 122 were constructed, it's believed that over 400,000 would have been needed to protect the entire nation. This is due to the fact that the Vaults were never truly intended to save humanity; rather, they were social experiments being conducted by the United States government. Each of these experiments varied in lethality, complexity, and cruelty, often ending in disaster and causing the collapse of their respective Vaults, with few exceptions.

Starting from the series' first entry, each installment takes the player character protagonist to different locations around the landscape of the former United States, from mere decades to entire centuries after the nuclear apocalypse, observing the evolution of nature, the transformation of human civilization, and the efforts by people and groups of differing beliefs and ideologies to restore or destroy what remains."
